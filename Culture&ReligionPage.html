ISRAEL: CULTURE AND RELIGION

Explore the rich culture and diverse religions of Israel, from ancient traditions to modern practices.

CULTURE

Israel’s culture is deeply rooted in the Jewish religion. In Israel, there are many Jewish immigrants from several different countries. As a result, Israel has a dynamic, creative and diverse culture. The holidays and festivals are all based on the Hebrew calendar.

Social Norms & Communication

Israelis are known for being straightforward, informal, and, at times, blunt or "prickly on the outside but sweet on the inside". They are often very close-knit and involved in each other's lives.

Family Orientation

Society is patriarchal and very family-centric, often including extended family members in daily life.

The "Sabra" Identity

The term "Sabra" refers to a Jew born in Israel, traditionally characterized as tough on the outside but soft on the inside, reflecting a resilient and direct nature.

Language

Hebrew is the official language, revived from biblical times, serving as a unifying force. Arabic is also used officially, and English is widely spoken.

Cuisine & Lifestyle

The culinary scene is a blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern, with falafel, hummus, and eggplant being staple foods. Lifestyle is generally relaxed and casual, yet highly energetic.

RELIGION

The majority of Israeli people practice Judaism, which accounts for 74.8% of the Israeli population. Islam is the second most prevalent religion at 17.6%. Christianity, Druze and other religions comprise 8.4% of the population.

Judaism

As of 2022, 73.5% of Israelis identify as Jewish. This includes a wide spectrum of practice, ranging from secular to traditional, liberal, and Ultra-Orthodox (Haredim).

Islam

The largest minority religion, practiced by about 18.1% of the population, with most identifying with the Sunni tradition.

Christianity

Comprises about 1.9% of the population, predominantly of Arab ethnicity.

Druze

The Druze community makes up about 1.6% of the population, primarily residing in Northern Israel.

Religious Freedom

Despite the strong Jewish character of the state, laws allow for complete religious freedom for all individuals.
