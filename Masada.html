Discover the History of Masada

Masada is a historic fortress located on a high plateau near the Dead Sea. It is known for its dramatic landscape and its important role in Jewish history. Visitors can explore ancient ruins, palaces, and fortifications while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding desert.

The Ancient Fortress of Masada

Masada is one of Israel’s most famous historical sites and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Tourists can hike or ride a cable car to the top of the fortress to see archaeological remains and learn about the dramatic events that took place there during ancient times.

4 Facts About Masada

1. Masada is an ancient fortress located on a high plateau near the Dead Sea.

2. It was built by Herod the Great as a royal palace and fortress.

3. Masada became famous for the historic siege by the Romans in 73–74 CE.

4. Today, Masada is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Israel’s most visited tourist attractions.
